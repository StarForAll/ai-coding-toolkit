# Normative Rules

* Rollback decisions must weigh current user impact, uncertainty, reversibility, and containment options explicitly.
* Ambiguous evidence should increase caution rather than justify optimistic continuation by default.
* Temporary recovery signals must not be mistaken for durable stabilization without supporting evidence.
* The cost of continuing forward under uncertainty must be compared against the cost and feasibility of rollback.
* When decision ownership is unclear, rollback decisioning must be treated as unresolved operational risk.
