# Normative Rules

* Material decisions should be recorded when future readers would otherwise need to reconstruct the reasoning from scattered context.
* A decision record must distinguish the chosen path, the evidence used, rejected alternatives, and expected consequences.
* Records should be concise enough to stay maintainable but specific enough to survive team, agent, or session changes.
* Decision logs must not falsely imply certainty where the decision was provisional or evidence was incomplete.
* When a prior decision is superseded, the newer record should make that relationship explicit.
