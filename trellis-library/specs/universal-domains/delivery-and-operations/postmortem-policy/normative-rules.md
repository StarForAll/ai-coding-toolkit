# Normative Rules

* Material incidents and repeat failures should produce a postmortem proportional to their impact and learning value.
* Postmortems must distinguish observed facts, inferred causes, and corrective decisions.
* The analysis should focus on system behavior, contributing conditions, and decision quality rather than scapegoating individuals.
* Corrective actions must be specific enough to track and verify later.
* A postmortem that does not change future verification, design, or operational behavior should be treated as incomplete.
