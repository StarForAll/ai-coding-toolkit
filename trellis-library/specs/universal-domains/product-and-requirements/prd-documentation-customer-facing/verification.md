# Verification

Check the following:

* The document uses plain, non-technical language that a business stakeholder can read without specialized knowledge
* All technical terms and acronyms are defined when first introduced
* Internal system names and engineering terminology are avoided or translated into business language
* Requirements are written from the customer's point of view, describing experiences and outcomes rather than implementation mechanics
* The document includes the minimum required sections: problem or opportunity, target users, business goals and success metrics, scope definition, solution overview, user requirements, delivery priorities, success criteria, and open questions
* Each requirement clearly explains why it matters to the customer or business
* Success criteria are measurable from the customer's viewpoint through observable workflow outcomes, service levels, or adoption expectations
* Core commitments are separated from future enhancements, and out-of-scope items are listed when confusion is likely
* Assumptions, dependencies, and pending decisions are explicitly stated
* The document does not contain API specifications, database schemas, class designs, or deployment details
* Examples or mockups clarify requirements without introducing hidden scope
