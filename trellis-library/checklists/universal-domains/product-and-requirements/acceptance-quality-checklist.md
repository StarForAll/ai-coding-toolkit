# Acceptance Quality Checklist

* Outcome-based criteria: acceptance criteria describe observable outcomes that can be verified through testing, observation, or measurement.
* Outcome-based criteria: criteria focus on what should happen, not how it should be implemented.
* Outcome-based criteria: success can be observed from the customer or end-user perspective.
* Outcome-based criteria: each criterion contributes to the intended business objective or user outcome.
* Scope clarity: current-scope items are clearly defined.
* Scope clarity: explicitly out-of-scope items are listed and justified.
* Scope clarity: core commitments, enhancements, and out-of-scope items are separated clearly.
* Scope clarity: boundaries between phases, releases, or versions are defined.
* Success and failure conditions: successful completion is clearly defined.
* Success and failure conditions: failure or rejection conditions are clearly defined.
* Success and failure conditions: partial-success or degraded-performance conditions are defined where applicable.
* Success and failure conditions: edge cases and boundary conditions are considered.
* Measurability and evidence: each criterion can be measured quantitatively where possible.
* Measurability and evidence: success can be demonstrated through evidence such as logs, metrics, or test results.
* Measurability and evidence: criteria do not rely on subjective judgment alone.
* Measurability and evidence: it is clear when a criterion passes or fails.
* Assumptions and dependencies: assumptions underlying the criteria are documented.
* Assumptions and dependencies: ambiguous requirements are flagged for clarification.
* Assumptions and dependencies: dependencies that affect acceptance are identified.
* Assumptions and dependencies: risks that might affect acceptance are noted.
