# Customer-Facing PRD Documentation

## Purpose

Define reusable rules for writing customer-facing product requirement documents (PRDs) that are easy for non-technical stakeholders to read while still making the intended scope and outcomes explicit.

## Applicability

Use this concern when creating or reviewing PRDs, proposals, or requirement summaries that will be shared directly with customers, business stakeholders, or other audiences who need clarity without implementation detail.

## Target Audience

This specification is designed for the following stakeholders:

- **Business Decision Makers**: Executives, managers, and department heads who need to understand business value and outcomes
- **Product Managers and Product Owners**: Professionals responsible for defining product strategy and requirements
- **Customer Representatives and End Users**: Individuals who will use the final product or service
- **Marketing and Sales Teams**: Professionals who need to understand product capabilities for positioning and sales
- **Project Stakeholders**: Anyone involved in project planning, execution, or oversight who requires clear understanding of deliverables

## Key Principles

1. **Customer Perspective Principle**: Always start from the customer experience and value proposition. Describe what matters to the customer, not how it will be implemented.

2. **Outcome-Oriented Principle**: Focus on measurable business outcomes and customer-visible results rather than implementation processes or technical details.

3. **Simplicity Principle**: Use plain, non-technical language that a business stakeholder can understand without specialized knowledge. Avoid jargon, acronyms, and technical terms unless absolutely necessary.

4. **Clarity Principle**: Ensure every requirement is clear, unambiguous, and actionable. Use specific language rather than vague generalizations.

5. **Scope Boundary Principle**: Clearly distinguish what is included in the current scope, what is planned for future phases, and what is explicitly out of scope.
