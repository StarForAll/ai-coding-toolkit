# Normative Rules

* Work must be assigned a risk tier before completion claims are made on non-trivial changes.
* Risk tiers must reflect blast radius, reversibility, uncertainty, data impact, security impact, and coordination complexity.
* Higher-risk tiers must trigger stronger review, evidence, and rollback expectations.
* When evidence is insufficient to justify a lower tier, the higher plausible tier must be used.
* Risk tiering decisions must be revisited when scope or implementation assumptions change materially.
